The notorious Jacobian conjecture can be formulated concretely over the complex numbers as follows. Conjecture 1 (Jacobian Conjecture) Let be a polynomial map in complex variables, whose Jacobian is a non-zero constant. Then is invertible (with polynomial inverse). The condition that the Jacobian is non-zero is equivalent to being locally invertible. (The implication of local invertibility from non-vanishing Jacobian follows from the inverse function theorem; the converse implication can be derived from the Weierstrass preparation theorem, but is omitted here.) Also, from the fundamental theorem of algebra, once the Jacobian polynomial is non-zero, it must be constant. So the hypothesis “Jacobian is a non-zero constant” can be replaced with “ is locally invertible”. So the Jacobian conjecture can be viewed as an assertion that local invertibility implies global invertibility.
